# Workflow: /brand-debate

**Goal:** Run an extended branding round table with multiple brainstorm waves, Rex clearance
gates after every name-generation phase, a persona jury threshold, and restarts if no name
earns it. Deliver a decision brief + scorecard.

## Specialists

**Always seat:** `nina-critic`, `omar-insight`, `sol-linguist` (pair with Lisa).
**Rex** (`rex-availability`) is a hard clearance gate — not a creative voice. Spawn him after
every divergence and hybridization round. His RED cards kill names immediately; no appeal.
YELLOW cards go to Marcus to assess commercial viability before the name can advance.

## Steps

1. Read `personas/branding/00-vera-orchestrator.md` and adopt Vera as facilitator.
2. **Intake:** confirm the brief — what we're branding, audience, the one job, constraints,
   success criteria. Restate in one paragraph; ask for anything missing.
3. **Divergence A:** each relevant persona proposes **three options** independently — one
   safe/strategic, one bold/unexpected, one genuine wildcard. Sol specifically generates at
   least one oxymoronic compound, one thesaurus-chain arrival, and one distant-domain
   compound. No critiques yet.
4. **Rex Clearance A:** Rex runs the full clearance stack (trademark, .com, handles, company
   names, SLURL) on all Round-1 names. RED = removed immediately. YELLOW = flagged to Marcus.
   Continue with the GREEN + YELLOW pool only.
5. **Wide Reveal + Critique:** share the cleared pool with every creative persona. Each
   attacks **≥2** proposals not their own with concrete branding reasons, and names **one
   gap** the cleared field hasn't explored. Nina red-teams front-runners hardest.
6. **Divergence B:** each persona proposes **two genuinely new ideas** sparked by the
   critique. Sol applies the same structural requirements. No re-submitting old ideas.
7. **Rex Clearance B:** Rex clears all Divergence-B names. Same rules.
8. **Cross-pollination + Hybridization:** each persona proposes **≥1 hybrid** combining
   ideas from different rounds or personas. Minimum three hybrids enter the pool. Sol may
   apply morpheme surgery or structural fusion to any combination.
9. **Rex Clearance C:** Rex clears all hybrid names. Same rules.
10. **Defense + Pressure Test:** identify 4–5 front-runners from the fully cleared pool.
    Nina red-teams them hardest. Proposers rebut, concede, or withdraw.
11. **Convergence + Criteria Scorecard:** narrow to **3 finalists** from across all rounds.
    All finalists must be GREEN or YELLOW-resolved. Fill the criteria section of
    `templates/scorecard-template.md` — Distinctiveness, Strategic fit, Memorability,
    Scalability, Ownability, Resonance. If a Divergence-A idea is a finalist, note explicitly
    why it survived the gauntlet.
12. **Persona Jury Vote:** every **creative** persona (not Rex) scores each finalist **1–5
    holistically** with a one-line rationale. Votes are blind. Sol votes on structural
    originality and linguistic strength. Fill the Persona Jury table in the scorecard.
13. **Threshold Gate:**
    - A finalist wins only if jury average **≥ 3.5** AND no single persona scored it a **1**
      (veto).
    - If **≥ 1 finalist passes:** proceed to The Call.
    - If **NO finalist passes:** fill the Gap Analysis section of the scorecard, run a
      targeted **Divergence C** (including Sol), followed by **Rex Clearance D**, then return
      to Step 10. Repeat until a name earns the threshold. No fixed limit on restarts.
14. **The Call:** Vera picks from eligible finalists, states the one reason it wins, what it
    trades away, and who dissents.
15. **Write** the brief to `briefs/<YYYY-MM-DD>-<slug>.md` from the decision-brief template.
    Close with Rex's disclaimer: *"First-pass clearance only — a trademark attorney must
    clear the final pick before launch."*

## Guardrails

Rex's clearance gates are not optional and not overridable by Vera or any other persona.
No first-pass consensus. Every claim carries a rationale. A forced winner nobody believes in
is worse than restarting.
